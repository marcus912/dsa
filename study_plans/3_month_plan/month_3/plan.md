# Month 3: Advanced Algorithms & Optimization

**Goal:** Master complex graph traversals and optimization techniques. This month separates good candidates from great ones.

## Week 9: Graphs (BFS, DFS, Topological Sort)
Model relationships and traverse networks. Understand state representation.
*   Number of Islands (ID: 200)
*   Clone Graph (ID: 133)
*   Course Schedule (ID: 207) - *Topological Sort*
*   Word Ladder (ID: 127) - *Hard, BFS*
*   Alien Dictionary (ID: 269) - *Hard*

## Week 10: Dynamic Programming (1D & 2D)
Learn to break down problems into overlapping subproblems and memoize/tabulate results.
*   Climbing Stairs (ID: 70)
*   Coin Change (ID: 322)
*   Longest Increasing Subsequence (ID: 300)
*   Longest Common Subsequence (ID: 1143)
*   Regular Expression Matching (ID: 10) - *Hard*

## Week 11: Backtracking & Greedy
Explore all combinations (Backtracking) vs. making the locally optimal choice (Greedy).
*   Subsets (ID: 78)
*   Permutations (ID: 46)
*   Combination Sum (ID: 39)
*   N-Queens (ID: 51) - *Hard*
*   Jump Game (ID: 55)

## Week 12: Review & Technical Evaluation
Focus on speed, communication, and tackling unseen problems under time pressure.
*   Revisit any weak areas from Months 1-3.
*   Do a mock review 2-3 times this week. Use a timer (45 minutes per problem).
*   Focus on communicating your thought process out loud before writing code.
*   **Key Hard Problems to review:** Trapping Rain Water (ID: 42), Minimum Window Substring (ID: 76), Serialize and Deserialize Binary Tree (ID: 297).
